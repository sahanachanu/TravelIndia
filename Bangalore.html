Places- Must Visit
Bangalore Palace
Why visit this?

This late 19th-century structure was used by both British officers and Indian royalty as a home and a place of business. It was the private residence of the royal Wodeyar family. The palace grounds are a popular venue for rock concerts and other music events..

What attracts the most??

The palace features mainly wooden interiors and is surrounded by gardens. The architectural style is a mix of Tudor and Scottish Gothic with many physical elements inside imported from Britain. Entering the palace on the first floor, note the distinctly Tudor interior design. Wooden beams, relief paintings and neo-classic floral carvings adorn the walls.
On the second floor, go into Durbar Hall, where the king once addressed his noble assembly. This bright interior is given a royal aura by columns, chandeliers and ornamental mirrors.Before heading back downstairs, explore rooms filled with works of art. Of the painting collection, several Greek and Dutch pieces have been here since the 19th century.

Entry Fee and Time

Foreign Tourist: Rs.460/-
India Citizens: Rs.230-
Bangalore Palace is open from 10am.to 5pm. Usually people spend 1 to 2 hours at this place.

Isckon Temple
Why visit this?

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) Temple was opened in 1997 and is visited by thousands of devotees every day. It is one of the largest temples of its kind in the world. This large and lavishly decorated temple complex dedicated to a Hindu god. Marvel at golden shrines, murals and hundreds of statues.

What attracts the most??

The architecture of Bangalore's ISKCON temple is a magnificent presence and is credited to being the largest ISKCON complexes in the world.

The most distinctive feature of this temple is its gold plated flag post which stands at a height of 17 metres, followed by its gilded Kalash Shikharas which is at a height of 8.5 metres. An example of neo - classical style of architecture, the ISKCON temple is embellished with the most exquisite style of stone carvings

The ISKCON temple complex has an expansive verdant garden and an amphitheatre for conducting various cultural programmes as well.

Entry Fee and Time

Timings: 7.15am. to 1.00 pm. and 4pm. to 8.20pm., Open on all days.

There is No Entry Fee. Usually people spend one hour at this place(hoping there is no queue for darshan)

Mysore Palace
Why visit this?

Mysore Palace also known as Amba Vilas Palace located at the heart of Mysore city is the most prominent tourist destination of Mysore that attracts millions of visitors round the year. This historical palace housed within the Old Fort facing the Chamunda Hills stands as the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty who ruled the Kingdom of Mysore from 1399 to 1950. Displaying Indo-Saracenic style of architecture, this sprawling building is a replacement of the old wooden building destroyed by fire in late 19th century. Commissioned by the Krishnarajendra Wadiyar IV, this palatial building comprises of two durbar halls, a number of colossal courtyards, buildings and strikingly beautiful gardens that speak volumes of the splendour of the Wadiyars.

What attracts the most??

The Mysore Palace, the seat of the famous Wodeyar Maharaja's of the Mysore Kingdom today stands as one of the precious possessions of the nation which is presently converted into a museum. The strikingly embellished and finely chiselled doors lead one to richly and elegantly ornate rooms. The exquisitely columned Durbar Hall, the solid silver doors, the finely incised mahogany ceilings and many other embellishments of the palace make one spellbound while giving an idea of the exuberant lifestyle of the royals. Displays in the palace include royal dresses, souvenirs, musical instruments and weapons used by the Wodeyars. An array of superb paintings including the ones illustrating 8 manifestations of Goddess Shakthi as also a masterpiece by famed artist Raja Ravi Verma finds place in the palace.

Transporation Modes in Bengaluru
Metro Trains
It is fast, efficient, fairly priced and environmentally friendly.Currently there are 2 lines around the city covering many stations. A route information is available at Metro Stops/Stations.. A fare calculator is available at Metro Fares..
Buses
The buses are usually overcrowded with little sitting room, and very hot if not air conditioned. They are however cheap and service many routes.The bus routes with bus numbers can be found at Bus Route.
Taxi/Cab
The most comfortable way to get around Bangalore is by taxi, however this is the most expensive option as well. Look for Ola Cabs at Taxi/Cabs. Look for Uber Cabs at Taxi/Cabs.
Auto Rikshaws
Auto is another form of transportation which are cheap and convenient to travel in. Around 3-4 people can sit in one auto with a driver.Autos can be found anywhere. Minimum auto fare starts at Rs.25/-
Climate in Bengaluru

WINTER - December to February (minimum is 10 deg C)
Monsoon - June to August
